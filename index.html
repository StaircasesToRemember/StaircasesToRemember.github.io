40 Staircases You MUST Visit Before You Die

What could be so special about staircases?

So many things could make a staircase unforgettable: it might feature a striking or beautiful design, serve as the site of a pivotal historical moment, stand as a forgotten relic of an ancient civilization, or exist as a modern spectacle shaped by contemporary design and pop culture. Whether it climbs thousands of feet into the sky or only rises a mere six or seven steps, there is only one way to fully experience what makes each of these staircases so special.

The Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China runs across northern China, stretching thousands of miles from the eastern coast near the Bohai Sea to the deserts of the west. Construction began as early as the 7th century BCE and continued for over two thousand years, with major rebuilding during the Qin, Han, and Ming dynasties. Built to defend against invasions from the north, the wall served as a military barrier, a lookout system, and a means of controlling movement and trade.
